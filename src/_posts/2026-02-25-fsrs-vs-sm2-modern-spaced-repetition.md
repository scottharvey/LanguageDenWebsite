If you're serious about language learning, you've probably heard of spaced repetition. It's the scientifically-proven method that shows you flashcards right before you're about to forget them. But did you know that the algorithm powering your spaced repetition app can make a massive difference in your learning efficiency?

## The Problem with SM-2

Most flashcard apps, including the popular Anki, use an algorithm called SM-2 (SuperMemo 2). Created in 1987 by Piotr Wozniak, SM-2 was revolutionary for its time. But that was nearly 40 years ago—before we had smartphones, machine learning, or decades of additional memory research.

SM-2 has several limitations:

- **Fixed intervals**: It uses predetermined time intervals that don't adapt to your personal learning patterns
- **One-size-fits-all**: Everyone gets the same spacing, regardless of how their memory actually works
- **No learning from mistakes**: If you consistently forget certain types of words, SM-2 doesn't adjust its approach

## Enter FSRS: The Future of Spaced Repetition

FSRS (Free Spaced Repetition Scheduler) represents a modern approach to spaced repetition. Instead of using fixed formulas from the 1980s, FSRS uses machine learning to adapt to your unique forgetting curve.

Here's what makes FSRS special:

### 1. Personalization That Actually Works

FSRS learns from every review session. Struggle with verb conjugations? It'll show them more frequently. Ace your nouns? You'll see them less often. This isn't just a minor tweak—it's a fundamental rethinking of how spaced repetition should work.

### 2. Scientifically Superior Predictions

Recent studies comparing FSRS to SM-2 show that FSRS predicts your likelihood of remembering a card with 15-20% better accuracy. In practical terms, this means:

- Fewer unnecessary reviews of cards you know well
- Better-timed reviews for cards you're struggling with
- More efficient use of your study time

### 3. Real-World Impact

Let's put this in perspective. If you're learning 1000 Spanish words:

- **With SM-2**: You might do 50-60 reviews per day
- **With FSRS**: You could achieve the same retention with 30-40 reviews
- **Time saved**: 30-40% reduction in daily review time

That's potentially hours saved each week—time you can spend actually using the language instead of grinding through flashcards.
